Banaue Rice Terraces

What are the Banaue Rice Terraces?

The Banaue Rice Terraces are ancient agricultural terraces carved into the mountains of Ifugao province in the Philippines over 2,000 years ago by the indigenous Ifugao people. They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World."

What makes it a popular tourist spot?

What makes the Banaue Rice Terraces a popular tourist spot is a combination of their historical, cultural, and natural significance. Visitors are drawn to the breathtaking scenic beauty, offering panoramic views and trekking opportunities, as well as the chance to engage with the local Ifugao community and learn about their traditional practices.

How do I get to the Banaue Rice Terraces?

To reach the Banaue Rice Terraces, you can take a bus from Manila to Banaue, which is approximately an 8-9 hour journey. Several bus companies operate this route, offering both daytime and overnight trips.

What is the best time to visit the Banaue Rice Terraces?

The best time to visit is during the planting season (April to May) or the harvest season (June to July and October to November) when the terraces are lush and green or golden with ripe rice.

Where can I stay while visiting the Banaue Rice Terraces?

There are several accommodations ranging from budget-friendly guesthouses to more comfortable hotels in Banaue town. Some popular options include Banaue Homestay, Banaue Hotel, and Bogah Homestay.

What activities can I do at the Banaue Rice Terraces?

  • Trekking: Explore various trails and routes around the terraces.
  • Cultural Tours: Visit traditional Ifugao villages and learn about their way of life.
  • Photography: Capture the breathtaking landscapes and terraces.
  • Interaction with Locals: Participate in cultural activities and learn traditional farming techniques.
